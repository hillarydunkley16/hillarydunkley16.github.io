In my first semester of my first year at Smith, I took an Introduction to Computer Science class. In this class, I coded in Python on Replit, and created a lot of fun programs.

Here are some of them:

Cash Withdrawal

This was one of the first asssignements I did in class. In this program, the user is asked to enter a cash amount, and then gives the user how many $20s, $10s, $5s and $1s the amount goes into. This project helped me learn the basics of Python, such as the input, int, and print function.

Link to the Repl

Pig Latin Translator

For this assignment, the program asks the user for a word, which is then translated into Pig Latin. This assignment was good practice in the use of lists, slices and if-statements.

Link to the Repl

Calculator

This assigment was very fun to figure out! It was one of my first times using graphics, which was challenging at first, but eventually I got the hang of it. You can add, subtract, multiply and divide with any two numbers.

Link to the Repl

The Final Project: a Pacman Game

For the final project, my partner (Kate Berklau-Halvor '23) and I created a rudimentary Pacman game, with a maze, a pacman, a ghost, pellets and a score counter. For the ghost, we used AI to determine the ghost's movement based on the location of the pacman on the map. It was very fun to collaborate with another person on a longer term project, and we enjoyed presenting it in class.
